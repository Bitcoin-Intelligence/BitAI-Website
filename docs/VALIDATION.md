# LocalAGI Network — Validation Feedback (2026-03-27)

## What it is
Two-sided P2P marketplace where AI agents run on local devices. Desktop app serves both roles:
- **Neurons:** run agentic AI tasks locally on their own hardware (privacy-first)
- **Users:** submit tasks to the network (cheaper, uncensored)

## Verdict: Validated enough to proceed

Working product exists (desktop app + P2P network). Real market gap identified.

## The market gap

No existing product checks all four boxes: agentic tasks + privacy + easy-to-use + uncensored.
- Ollama/LM Studio: chat only, no agentic tasks (data analysis, automation, image/video)
- ComfyUI: too technical for average users
- ChatGPT/Claude: censored, data leaves your device
- **Local AGI fills the gap** by bundling sandboxed agents + AI models into one easy app that runs on consumer hardware (Apple Silicon)

## GTM strategy
- **Supply-side first:** recruit Neurons (privacy-conscious users running locally)
- Neurons use the app for their own tasks, so they retain even without User demand
- Spare compute leasing is a natural upsell, not a cold-start problem
- Landing page should speak to BOTH sides (it's a marketplace, the network is the product)
- But marketing/distribution effort should target Neurons first

## Landing page recommendations
1. **Lead with outcomes, not architecture.** "Your AI, your device, your rules" beats "AI, powered by people"
2. **Address both audiences in the hero.** Neuron hears "nothing leaves your computer." User hears "use the network when you need more power."
3. **Reduce use cases from 7 to 1-2 hero demos** that make people feel the product
4. **Move network stats lower** — they're proof the network works, not the opening pitch
5. **Ship the download button.** "Download App" currently goes nowhere. Every day without a funnel is wasted traffic.
6. **Add email capture** if app isn't ready for public download yet

## Positioning advice
- **Lead with privacy + agentic capability**, not price. "Cheaper" is weak positioning because cloud AI prices drop fast.
- **Let uncensored/NSFW be discovered, not headlined.** Real demand exists but leading with it repels partners/press/investors.
- **The moat is ease-of-use + agentic layer**, not just "we allow NSFW" or "we're cheaper"

## Key metrics to track now
1. **Downloads per week** — is the message landing?
2. **Tasks completed per user per week** — is the product sticky?

Low downloads = messaging/distribution problem. Low tasks = product problem. Both fixable.
