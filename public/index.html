The Local AGI network is powered by high-performance devices running the Local AGI desktop app. These devices provide intelligence for the entire platform, allowing less powerful devices to utilize Local AGI agents when they lack the resources to run local AI models.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

What is Local AGI?

Local AGI is a private AI agent app and a peer-to-peer network. You run AI agents on your own hardware to analyze data, create images and video, automate tasks, and run research workflows — drawing on a network of devices (Neurons) instead of a central data center.

What problem does it solve?

Most AI tools run in the cloud, which raises privacy, cost, usage-limit, and control concerns. Local AGI gives you a local-first way to delegate real work to AI agents while keeping more control over your data and how tasks run.

Who is Local AGI for?

Creators, analysts, founders, agencies, operators, and privacy-conscious users who want AI agents for research, content, data analysis, automation, image and video creation, and business workflows.

What can Local AGI do?

It covers use cases across data analysis, stock and crypto analysis, image creation and editing, video creation, deep research, and administrative automation — like the examples shown above.

Is Local AGI private?

Local AGI is local-first: local AI models run on your device and agents work in a secure sandbox. You control when external connectors or web sources are used, so sensitive work can stay on your machine.

Why not just use ChatGPT or Claude?

Those are excellent general assistants. Local AGI is for people who want local-first agents, private workflows, saved artifacts, and task automation that runs from their own machine.

Is it fully reliable?

AI agents still need human verification — especially for live news, finance, and legal topics. Local AGI surfaces evidence, confidence levels, and source links so you can review claims before acting on them.

Does it replace people?

No. It works best as an assistant that drafts, researches, summarizes, and prepares work for your review.

What is a Neuron, and how do they earn rewards?

A Neuron is a device on the network that contributes spare compute to run models and agents for others. From the same app you can switch into “Provide” mode to run tasks and earn — while keeping things private and on-device.

Which platforms are supported?

The desktop app is available for macOS today, with Windows and Linux coming soon.

How do I get started?

Download the app, give an agent a task, and review the results. That’s it.
